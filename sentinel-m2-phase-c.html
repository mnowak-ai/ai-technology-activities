4Step back

Think through the tradeoffs

Before you write your final report, sit with these four questions. Your shift decisions are the right context for them.

What happens if Sentinel escalates too often?
Who gets pulled in? What's the cost to the team — and to the users waiting for help?
What happens if Sentinel misses an early warning sign?
What could a small ignored issue grow into — especially on a system that hospitals or schools depend on?
How do you decide when you have "enough" information to act?
All three cases are still uncertain. At what point is uncertainty okay — and at what point does it require escalation?
What role should AI play in these final decisions?
AI recommended monitoring for all three. You may disagree with one or more. What does that tell you about AI's limitations in live support work?
Model answer

The hardest tradeoff was Case 2 — the likely explanation is mundane, but real people are still blocked from doing their jobs right now, so I couldn't justify waiting even though I didn't think it was a security threat. Where AI and I diverged, it was usually because the AI didn't have the workplace context (the deadline, the policy rollout) that made a calmer explanation more likely — it's better at generating possibilities than judging which one actually fits the situation.

"

Operational judgment is about balancing uncertainty — not eliminating it.
