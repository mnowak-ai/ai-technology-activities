Sentinel Technical Support & Operations · Incident Timeline · End-of-Shift Export
INC-0241 System Performance Slowdown — Government Services Portal

Reports of sluggish system performance began arriving at the support desk around 9:20 AM. The Government Services Portal was loading more slowly than usual — pages that typically respond in a few seconds were taking up to a minute or more. Dashboard data was also delayed. The support team noted that today was the final day of the monthly reporting period, which historically brings in a higher-than-normal number of users logging in simultaneously. By mid-morning, the user count had risen to over 60 — significantly above the typical daily peak of around 40. No error messages were reported, and the system never went offline.

Performance improved gradually through the late morning and appeared to return to normal by approximately 12:10 PM, without any action from the support team. The cause was not formally identified. High user volume during the deadline period is the working assumption — but this has not been confirmed through system data.

INC-0242 Login & Access Issues — Hospital Records System

A second issue was reported by the hospital records team starting around 11:45 AM. Three employees stated they were unable to log in using their usual passwords. One of those employees (identified in logs as U-118) attempted to log in five times before gaining access. Another employee reported that their password "just stopped working" that morning. The system remained fully operational throughout — other users across the hospital were logged in and working without any problems.

Context noted during the investigation: Sentinel had updated its sign-in process the previous week, introducing new password requirements. The announcement was sent by email. The support desk received no confirmation that all affected users had read it or updated their credentials. By mid-afternoon, most affected users had regained access. Whether the issue was directly caused by the password update has not been confirmed.

INC-0243 Unexpected System Behavior — School District File System

The third incident came in around 2:30 PM. School district users began experiencing an intermittent error when saving files — the message "Action could not be completed" appeared, but only for some users and only some of the time. Two users in the same department reported opposite experiences during the same time window: one encountered the error repeatedly, the other had no issues at all. No related errors appeared in system-wide logs.

Context: Two days prior, the school district's file storage had been reorganized — folders were moved to new locations. The support team noted that not all user accounts had been updated to reflect the new folder structure. Whether this caused the error has not been confirmed. The frequency of the error appeared to decrease through the afternoon. By end-of-shift, the error could not be reproduced. The underlying cause remains open and unresolved.

Handoff note — end of shift · Support Team → Knowledge Operations

Three items need documentation before they're filed. The Government Services Portal slowdown this morning (INC-0241) has resolved and is the most straightforward to document. High user volume during the deadline period is the working explanation — but it was never technically confirmed, so document it as "likely" rather than "confirmed." The SOP should include a note to monitor user counts during future end-of-month periods, since this pattern may repeat.

The hospital records login issue (INC-0242) is trickier. Most users are back in, but the root cause hasn't been established. We believe the new password requirements are to blame — the timing and the fact that only a small number of accounts were affected lines up with that. But we can't say it for certain. Do not document it as a confirmed cause. Watch for repeat login failures over the next few days; if it keeps happening after users have had time to update their credentials, something else may be going on. The school district file error (INC-0243) is the one I'm least confident about. The error was intermittent and we couldn't reproduce it by end-of-shift. The folder reorganization two days ago is our best guess — it would explain why only some accounts were affected — but that's still a guess. Document the uncertainty clearly and flag that the cause is unresolved. If similar errors come in tomorrow, escalate rather than monitoring.
