Like a hospital monitor tracking a patient's heartbeat — if something changes, it shows on screen and someone takes a look. We do the same, except the "patient" is a digital system.

2

Your role on the team

You're a Security Monitoring Analyst — the starting role every analyst holds. You read activity reports (records of what a system has been doing) and notice anything that looks off.

What you do

→Watch activity reports during your shift.
→Notice anything unusual or unexpected.
→Take a closer look to understand it.
→Decide whether it needs more attention.

Handled by others

×Repairing the systems themselves.
×Making big decisions on your own.
×Handling serious problems alone.
×Having every answer on day one.

In short: you are the first set of eyes. Notice things early, pass along what looks important — and grow into it shift by shift.

3

How work happens here

Every shift follows the same simple rhythm.

1

Watch

Activity reports come in all shift. Most of what you see is completely normal.

2

Spot

Something stands out — an odd sign-in time, or a sudden change in how busy things are.

3

Look closer

Check the details and look for a pattern. One-time thing, or part of something bigger?

4

Decide

Choose how to respond. You'll learn the three choices just below.

"

Not everything unusual is dangerous — but everything unusual should be checked.

4

What you'll see

Common things that show up in reports. None is automatically a problem — just worth a look.

Unusual sign-ins

Someone signs in from far away, or at an odd hour like the middle of the night.

System errors

A part of a system reports that something didn't work the way it should.

A sudden spike

Far more requests arrive at once than the system normally handles.

Access changes

Someone can suddenly open files they couldn't reach before, with no clear reason.

A slowdown

A usually-fast system starts responding much more slowly than normal.

Repeated failed sign-ins

The same account tried over and over with the wrong password.

5

How decisions work

After a closer look, you'll pick one of three responses.

Ignore
It's normal

Everyday, expected behavior. Nothing here needs follow-up.

Monitor
Keep watching

Not clearly a problem, but worth noting to see if it continues.

Escalate
Send it up

Important enough that the senior team should review it.

"

There is no perfect answer — just good reasoning.

6

Working with AI tools

AI helps you work faster — but know exactly what it does, and what it doesn't.

SummarizesPulls a long report down to the key points fast.

Spots patternsFlags things that repeat or look like past events.

Can be wrongIt misses things sometimes. Treat it as a suggestion.

You decideA human always reviews and makes the final call.

"

AI is a support tool, not the decision-maker.

7

Before your first mission

You don't need to know everything today. Every analyst here started right where you are. The skills that matter — paying attention, looking closely, explaining your thinking — grow with practice.

In Mission 1, you'll read real-style activity reports, decide what stands out, and choose how to respond. Take your time and trust your reasoning.
