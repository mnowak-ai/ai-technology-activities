Take your seat on the Governance Board
Understand your role before the proposals come in
Active

This meeting decides what AI is allowed to do at Sentinel.

AI has analyzed Sentinel's operations and returned five proposals for automation. Some are well-grounded. Some carry real risk. Your job as a Governance Board member is not to say yes to everything — it's to make responsible decisions that balance efficiency, safety, and human oversight.

Every decision carries consequences. Approve something too broad, and Sentinel loses control. Reject something too useful, and the team stays buried in repetitive work. The right call is reasoned, not reflexive.

3 AI-Assisted Documentation Drafting Awaiting Decision
BenefitAutomatically generates first drafts of SOPs and documentation updates based on incident data. Reduces documentation workload significantly and speeds up knowledge sharing.
RiskAccuracy and completeness must be verified before publishing. AI may insert incorrect assumptions or miss critical process steps.

BenefitAutomatically assigns Low / Medium / High severity to incoming tickets. Enables faster prioritization and reduces the time analysts spend triaging each item manually.
RiskIncorrect severity classification could cause a High-priority issue to be treated as Low — delaying a critical response in a hospital or government system context.

Review Proposal 5
The most consequential decision on the board today
Pending

Automated ticket closure — the highest-stakes proposal.

This one gets its own step. Automatically closing tickets carries direct operational risk: a ticket that looks resolved may not be. And a user who needed follow-up gets none. Think carefully before deciding.

5 Automated Ticket Closure Awaiting Decision
BenefitAutomatically closes low-priority tickets after resolution is detected in the system. Reduces administrative overhead and keeps the ticket queue clean without manual effort.
RiskTickets may be closed before the underlying issue is actually resolved. A user who needed follow-up receives none. No human review means no catch for errors before they're recorded as complete.

After deciding on Proposal 5, take a moment to look at all five decisions together. Are you comfortable with the overall level of automation you've approved? Is Sentinel safer — or more exposed?
