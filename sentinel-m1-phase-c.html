2Your tools

The decision framework

Three choices, every time. Keep them in mind as you work through each event below.

Ignore

Activity appears normal. No further action needed right now.

Monitor

Activity is unusual but not urgent. Keep a close eye and check again next shift.

Escalate

Activity may signal a real problem. Send it to senior analysts for investigation.

4Think it through

The tradeoffs of every decision

Before you finalize your calls, sit with these three questions. There's no wrong answer here — just honest thinking.

What happens if you ignore something that turns out to be important?
Who might be affected? What could you have done differently?
What happens if you escalate something that turns out to be completely normal?
What's the cost? Is it better to be safe, or does over-escalating cause its own problems?
How do you stay careful without overreacting to everything?
What does good judgment look like when you're not sure?
Model answer

Ignoring something real means the damage happens before anyone reacts — in a hospital system, that could mean patient data exposure. Escalating something normal wastes senior analysts' time and can cause "alert fatigue," where real threats get missed later because everything looks urgent. Good judgment means using context — like whether a pattern repeats, or whether the system involved is high-stakes — rather than reacting to every flag the same way.

"

Good analysts are careful — not alarmist.
